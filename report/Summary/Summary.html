Sasha Buskin When conducting this testing report, I had a role in usability data collection (interview questions) and usability data presentation.

When reflecting on the testing itself, we intended to follow iterative and informal principles (Dumas, 1999). This ensured a clear and relaxed environment in which the participants conducted their usability testing, providing more precise product feedback and data.

Participant selection also had to be considered for this specific type of product. Unlike paid software, a website is a free-to-access product, which brings a unique set of standards. Furthermore, due to the universal nature of the Internet, a target market can be demographically broad, which also presents a broad range of expectations to consider. Additionally, diversity in web browsing hardware is extensive, resulting in a variety of website experiences (A.Edmonds, 2003).

This was my first practical usability testing experience (AUT & industry), and when considering my software development major, this report demonstrated the value that usability testing gives to a client or development team. Usability testing is necessary to create a system that works with, not against, the user.
