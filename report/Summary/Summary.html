Reflection on Process
What I did:
My team and I surveyed users of various age groups around us for reviews and experiences on specific websites and conducted usability studies on the interaction between well-designed and provided web pages. Usability testing was conducted with three participants from different classes and designed to examine the three criteria for web pages' efficiency, effectiveness, and satisfaction so that they could complete certain tasks.

How I did that:
Our team members began to conceptualize the results of the web page. It is important to identify the features of a webpage that are potentially useful or inconvenient to use. Therefore, we wrote a realistic scenario and based on the questionnaire, participants compared their familiar web pages with the web pages provided, looked at the responses, and conducted interviews.

As a software developer, I have conducted usability tests several times in previous academic courses. Therefore, I knew the direction and method of the basic test, and I prepared the questions before the test. We prepared many practical questions from the participant's points of view to make the participants participate more in the survey, and the participants did their job very well. Moreover, when I compared their feedback with the results I thought, I found that a lot of them matched. By conducting this usability test, I could synthesize more objective reviews of people and understand the differences, not just my thoughts about a particular object or environment.
Sasha Buskin When conducting this testing report, I had a role in usability data collection (interview questions) and usability data presentation.

When reflecting on the testing itself, we intended to follow iterative and informal principles (Dumas, 1999). This ensured a clear and relaxed environment in which the participants conducted their usability testing, providing more precise product feedback and data.

Participant selection also had to be considered for this specific type of product. Unlike paid software, a website is a free-to-access product, which brings a unique set of standards. Furthermore, due to the universal nature of the Internet, a target market can be demographically broad, which also presents a broad range of expectations to consider. Additionally, diversity in web browsing hardware is extensive, resulting in a variety of website experiences (A.Edmonds, 2003).

This was my first practical usability testing experience (AUT & industry), and when considering my software development major, this report demonstrated the value that usability testing gives to a client or development team. Usability testing is necessary to create a system that works with, not against, the user. An appealing layout design is crucial to the user experience, and not meeting the users’ standards can severely impede their overall experience.
Andre Hewitt For this usability test I was assigned the role of facilitating a participant through a selection of tasks while observing and collecting data on the usability of a website based on the participants experience.

To ensure the best results, I removed distractions and allowed my participant to fully engage while they attempted to interact with the webpage and its features. To help ensure reliable results, I did not tell my participant how to use the webpage as this would affect the accuracy of test results. Overall, I think our usability test was successful and if we were developing this website, I would have sufficient data to correct design flaws which according to our usability test would be detrimental to a user’s overall experience.
