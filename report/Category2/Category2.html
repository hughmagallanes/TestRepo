Anecdotal Research Data

This section of the report contains research questioning data that details the test participants' user experience. This research was conduceted on the participants in interview form after a 5-15 minute interaction with the website.

The bullet points below represent different participants answers to the same interview question.

What are you thinking as you first view this main page?

• It is a very confusing webpage design layout, but I see a lot of vehicles with numbers, so I guess it’s a vehicle selling website.

If you were looking for a toys section, where would you expect to find it?

• Well, I can see there is a menu on the side of the screen but it’s not English, and I can’t see a language changing option. I would just have to guess.

How was the experience of using the product?

• Disorienting and I didn’t like it.

[Probe:] How easy or difficult was it to navigate?

• Very difficult because the layout is unusual; I can see a menu but the rest is just a jumble of random vehicles and strange products.

[Probe:] What are your thoughts on the design and layout?

• Unusual, unappealing, confusing. It’s too disorganised and random.

[Internal] How long did it take the user to complete this task?

• 3 minutes.

How would you describe your overall experience with the product?

• It was a pretty poor experience overall, It took me a while to figure out how to use it, and the layout and design is one of the worst and confusing that I've ever seen.

What did you like the most about using this product?

• Well it was unique experience at least, but I can’t think of too many positives about the website.

What did you like the least?

• The layout and general organisation of the whole thing.

What, if anything, surprised you about the experience?

• I have never been on a website this badly designed and confusingly jumbled.

What, if anything, caused you frustration?

• The organisation and layout, and the fact that I could change the language to English to even read the tiny menu on the side.

On a scale from 1 to 5 (1 = not at all likely, 5 = very likely), how likely are you to recommend this product to a friend?

• 0, as it’s barely usable.
